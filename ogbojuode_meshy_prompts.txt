OGBOJU ODE 3D — MESHY PROMPT LIST
==================================
Matches GameObjects created in Assets/Editor/SceneSetupWizard.cs.
Generate in the priority order listed. Set Pose = T-Pose for any item
marked [RIGGED] since those need animation.

Recommended order: generate one at a time (not batch-pasted), check the
result before spending credits on the next, especially items 1-6 below.


1. Player_Akara_Ogun  [RIGGED — CHARACTER]
------------------------------------------
Full-body 3D game character, a legendary 19th-century Yoruba hunter.
Wearing traditional hunter's attire (Aso Ode), distressed brown woven
fabric covered in small leather amulets, cowrie shells, and gourd charms
strapped across the torso and arms. Dark skin, braided hair, determined
expression, carrying a machete sheath at the hip. Stylized dark fantasy
game-ready model, clean quad topology, T-pose, ready for auto-rigging.


2. Creature_Agbako  [BOSS CREATURE]
------------------------------------
A terrifying towering boss monster with sixteen small glowing eyes
arranged symmetrically across its head and upper body, skin like gnarled
dark tree bark fused with rock, long clawed arms, hulking posture.
Afro-surrealist dark fantasy creature, highly detailed, game-ready,
T-pose or idle stance.


3. Ostrich_King_Oba_Eye  [RIGGED — RIDDLE BOSS]
-------------------------------------------------
A surreal humanoid-bird hybrid entity: from the neck up, a regal, stern
human king wearing a weathered tribal crown; from the neck down, the
body, legs, and feathers of a massive ostrich. Mystical, eerie, dignified
bearing rather than aggressive. Stylized realism, game-ready, T-pose.


4. Creature_Ijamba
-------------------
A hulking brute giant from West African folklore, thick corded muscle,
dark reddish-brown rough hide like dried clay, small deep-set eyes,
oversized fists. Heavy, slow, intimidating stance. Game-ready creature
model, clean topology, dark fantasy style.


5. Creature_Eru
----------------
A shape-shifting forest spirit creature, multiple faces blurring across
one head as if seen mid-transformation, lean and wiry build, dark green
mossy skin with faint bioluminescent veins. Unsettling, fast-looking
silhouette, low-poly game-ready, dark fantasy style.


6. Ghommid_Spirit
------------------
A small mischievous forest spirit gnome, half-human half-plant hybrid,
skin made of green moss and twisting roots, glowing amber eyes, playful
crouched pose. Low-poly, optimized for a mobile game, whimsical
dark-fantasy style.


7. Musket (handheld item, distinct from Musket_Muzzle anchor in code)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Low-poly game prop, ancient West African flintlock musket. Dark mahogany
wooden stock intricately carved with geometric Yoruba tribal patterns and
small metallic protective charms inlaid along the barrel. Weathered iron
barrel, aged brass fittings. Clean topology for a handheld weapon prop.


8. Machete
-----------
Low-poly game prop, a heavy broad-bladed hunting machete with a worn
leather-wrapped handle, housed in a weathered leather sheath. Slightly
nicked blade suggesting long use. Game-ready, clean topology.


9. Clay_Hut (+ Thatched_Roof)

Modular 3D game prop, a traditional pre-colonial Yoruba mud hut with a

a village hub kit.


10. Carved_Pillar (Arugba)
----------------------------
Low-poly game prop, a tall carved wooden ceremonial pillar with faint

11. Mahogany_Tree  (x20 instances in Forest)
-----------------------------------------------
A single ancient West African mahogany tree, thick gnarled trunk with
exposed twisting roots, dark bark, dense canopy. Low-poly game asset
optimized for repeated instancing across a forest, game-ready, clean

12. Spirit_Fungus  (x15 instances, forest floor)
----------------------------------------------------
A small cluster of bioluminescent mystical fungi/mushrooms, soft glowing

Stylized dark fantasy prop, low-poly, game-ready, optimized for

13. Central_Bonfire  (village hub centerpiece)
--------------------------------------------------
A West African-style ceremonial bonfire pit — a stacked pile of dark

14. Defensive_Spike  (x10 instances, village perimeter)
------------------------------------------------------------
A single sharpened wooden defensive stake/spike, weathered dark wood,
driven into the ground at a slight outward angle. Low-poly game prop,
designed for repeated instancing along a perimeter line.


SKIP — DO NOT GENERATE ON MESHY
==================================
Forest_Floor / Village_Ground
  -> Terrain, not a discrete mesh. Use Unity Terrain tool + tiling
     ground texture instead.

TEMP_Bullet_Prefab (musket projectile)
  -> Too small/fast to read as a detailed mesh in motion. Use a plain
     sphere + particle trail effect instead.

Musket_Muzzle
  -> Empty anchor Transform in code, not a visible object.


NOTES
======
- Free tier: ~100 credits/month. Prioritize items 1-6 first (characters/
  creatures are highest risk of needing a re-prompt); batch items 11-14
  (tree, fungus, bonfire, spike) together since they're low-detail,
  low-risk "cheap win" props.
- Export format: FBX or GLB, both import cleanly into Unity.
- Items marked [RIGGED] should use Meshy's auto-rig feature after
  generation (T-pose export required for this to work).
- On import, rescale so Agbako reads as roughly 4x Akara-Ogun's height,
  matching the (3, 6, 3) scale already set in SceneSetupWizard.cs —
  Meshy/Tripo exports come in arbitrary units and won't match this
  automatically.
